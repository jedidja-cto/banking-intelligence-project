Glossary
========

This glossary defines key terms used across the Namibia Financial Systems
Intelligence Lab. Entries are intentionally concise and oriented towards how
the terms are used in this repository.

Financial system
----------------

The network of institutions, markets, infrastructures, and rules that enable
the creation, transfer, and storage of financial value in Namibia.

Bank
----

A regulated institution that accepts deposits, provides credit, and offers
payments and other financial services to individuals and organisations.

Regulator
---------

An authority responsible for setting and enforcing rules that govern parts of
the financial system, such as banks, payment systems, and capital markets.

Payment rail
------------

The infrastructure, rules, and processes that enable the transfer of funds
between participants (for example through clearing and settlement systems).

Module
------

A self-contained folder under `modules/` that focuses on a specific
institution or rail. Each module has a standard structure and documentation
set.

Synthetic data
--------------

Data that is artificially generated (not sourced from real customers or
transactions) and used to illustrate flows, edge cases, or scenarios without
exposing sensitive information.
